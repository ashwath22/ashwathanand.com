VENDOBOX

Team: Prof. Michael Felix, Ashwath Anand, Amar Chadgar, Jose Bertero, Eva dunn, Shreya Mehta, Alexandra patron, Aneesa L, Xioawen Xao, Chen Shuai, Aaron Mendoza
Duration: 10 weeks
My role: Electronics, Hardware, User Experience design and Mechanics


Challenge

VENDOBOX is a continuation of Vend-O-Bot, our overhaul of the previous machine to make it bigger and better in every aspect. For this update we focussed on improving the electronics, the hardware and branding and also introduced a digital screen experience. The whole rebarnding was focussed on making VENDOBOX a more fun and intimate experience to interact with.

For this version we decided to use a Raspberry Pi 2 as the brain driving the screen, 3 stepper motors, ultrasonic sensor and RFID reader. We created our own currency called vendocoin which was a hexagon saped coin with an RFID chip in it that would act as currency.

Process

The first stage involved learning to work the raspberry pi and migrating from the arduino to the pi. Lil' buddy, a housing for the pi with room for a screen, cables, a keyboard and mouse was created by Jose.


The following video shows some of the initial testing process. This includes running some initial tests with the RFId reader, the ultrasonic sensor, making the harware communicate with prototype GUI animations, testing motors and plenty of incremental iterations.






VENDOBOX was one of the biggest collaboration efforts I have ever been a part of. We had teams for Systems(My team) that took care of all the hardware and electronics, a mechanism team, a branding team and a builder team.

So all teams had to regularly work with other teams to make sure all our puzzle pieces fit together at the end. My team had to regularly work with the mechanism team to make sure the harware and mechanism work well together and also worked with the beanding teams to make sure the sequence of events and timings are matched with the hardware.





On a personal level, I learned how to work with teams filled with very talented people. My task at hand was very hard and it was only with the support of my Professor and team mates that I was able to successfully complete all my tasks and was able to even help other team mates.


Throughout we used Trello as a platform for collaboration where we coordinated work and kept tabs on all our deadlines.
